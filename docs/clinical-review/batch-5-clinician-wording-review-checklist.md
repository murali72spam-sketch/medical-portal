# Clinical Portal 2026 — Batch 5 Clinician Wording Review Checklist

## 1. Purpose

This Stage 3 checklist is a documentation-only aid for Dr. Murali's manual wording review of the selected Batch 5 parent-education resources. It is intended to help check whether existing parent-facing wording is clear, cautious, and safely bounded before any later wording-update or finalization work.

This document does not edit medical content, metadata, status, `medical_review_status`, or `last_reviewed`, and it does not mark any resource as reviewed.

## 2. Safety Boundary

- Use this checklist only for wording review.
- Do not add new medical advice during this stage.
- Do not add medication names, doses, dose intervals, maximum doses, treatment algorithms, diagnostic thresholds, product schedules, steroid instructions, dermatology treatment plans, imaging advice, orthotic prescriptions, fixed referral thresholds, crisis-management algorithms, safeguarding algorithms, eating-disorder pathways, or therapy pathways.
- Do not convert parent education pages into urgent-recognition, emergency, diagnostic, or treatment pages.
- Use "Needs clinician confirmation" wherever wording, clinical meaning, urgency level, reference adequacy, or parent action boundaries are uncertain.
- Keep caution high for Acne, Eczema, Physical Activity for Teenagers, Healthy Sleep Habits for Teenagers, Supporting Independence in Teenagers, and Back Pain and Posture because they contain safety-sensitive boundaries.
- Keep caution moderate-low for Flat Feet and Intoeing, while still checking pain, asymmetry, function, neurological, and referral-boundary wording.
