# Clinical Portal 2026 — Condition Visual Style Guide

## Purpose

This document defines the image and visual style system for Clinical Portal 2026.

The goal is to make every parent guide warm, professional, Indian-context appropriate, medically safe, and visually consistent.

The portal should use realistic, high-end parent-child images rather than generic icons, frightening medical photos, or graphic disease images.

---

## Core visual direction

Use this visual style consistently:

- Realistic high-end medical website photography
- Indian parent-child context
- Warm natural daylight
- Clean modern Indian home or paediatric clinic
- Calm, reassuring expressions
- Premium paediatric healthcare feel
- Soft teal, cream, coral, and warm neutral colour harmony
- No text inside images
- No logos or watermarks
- No medicine brand labels
- No frightening emergency scenes
- No graphic skin lesions, blood, needle close-ups, or severe distress

## Visual context categories

### 1. Mother-child home-care scene

Use for fever, cough, vomiting, diarrhoea, colic, sleep, newborn care, and general home-care topics.

Image idea:
Indian mother or parent caring for a child at home in a warm, calm, realistic setting.

### 2. Parent-child clinic scene

Use for vaccination, asthma inhaler education, growth assessment, thyroid/endocrine topics, chronic illness overview, and follow-up care topics.

Image idea:
Indian parent and child in a clean paediatric clinic setting.

### 3. Infection prevention / environment scene

Use for dengue, malaria, hand hygiene, safe food and water, travel health, and mosquito bite prevention.

Image idea:
Indian family demonstrating prevention behaviours such as removing stagnant water, using mosquito nets, handwashing, or safe food preparation.

### 4. Gentle skin-care scene

Use for eczema, hives, scabies, impetigo, ringworm, head lice, and molluscum.

Image idea:
Show supportive care or clinical review without graphic lesions.

### 5. Nutrition and feeding scene

Use for weaning, picky eating, breastfeeding, formula feeding, toddler nutrition, and adolescent nutrition.

Image idea:
Indian parent-child feeding context with familiar foods such as idli, dosa, dal, rice, curd, khichdi, banana, fruits, vegetables, and egg where appropriate.

### 6. Calm urgent-care guidance scene

Use for red flags, meningitis, encephalitis, sepsis awareness, neonatal danger signs, and severe breathing difficulty warning guides.

Image idea:
Parent holding or observing a child with gentle urgency. Avoid panic, ambulance drama, ICU imagery, tubes, blood, or frightening hospital scenes.

## Master image prompt template

Create a realistic, high-end photographic hero image for a paediatric parent guide on the topic "[TOPIC NAME]".

Show [VISUAL CONTEXT].

The image should feel trustworthy, calm, warm, and reassuring for Indian parents. Use realistic Indian family features, natural skin tones, soft daylight, clean composition, and a premium paediatric medical website style.

Include subtle context related to the topic: [TOPIC-SPECIFIC DETAILS].

Avoid frightening, dramatic, graphic, or hospital-emergency imagery. Do not include text, logos, watermarks, brand names, medicines with readable labels, needle close-ups, blood, severe distress, or identifiable real-person likeness.

Composition should work as a website hero image with breathing room for title text and responsive cropping.

## Safety rule

Images must support parent education and reassurance.

They must not imply diagnosis, individual treatment, medication dosing, emergency management, or guaranteed outcomes.
