Vaccination in Special Medical Conditions

Some children need individualized vaccine planning because of chronic illness, medicines, or immune-system concerns.

Special medical conditions do not mean vaccines are unimportant. Some vaccines may be especially important, while some may need timing, specialist review, or extra caution.

Who may need individualized planning?

Children with chronic illness, immunocompromise, heart disease, kidney disease, diabetes, neurological conditions, asplenia or splenic problems, and children receiving steroids or other immunosuppressive medicines may need a personalized vaccine plan.

Why planning matters

  • Some infections can be more severe in children with chronic illness.
  • Some vaccines may be especially important for protection.
  • Some vaccines may need timing around medicines, procedures, or specialist treatment.
  • Live vaccines need paediatrician or specialist review when immunosuppression is possible.

What parents should bring

  • Vaccination records and previous hospital discharge summaries.
  • Current medication list, including steroid or immune-suppressing medicines.
  • Specialist letters, diagnosis details, and recent blood test summaries if available.
  • Information about planned surgery, chemotherapy, biologic medicines, transplant care, or travel.

Important safety note

Do not use this guide to decide which vaccines are safe for a medically complex child. Your child's vaccine plan should be confirmed with your paediatrician and relevant specialist using the latest applicable IAP/ACVIP or national guidance.

Medical disclaimer

General education only This guide is parent education only and is not a vaccination decision tool. It does not replace specialist consultation, vaccine eligibility review, or individualized medical advice.
