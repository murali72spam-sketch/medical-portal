🫁 Croup in Children

Understanding the barking cough and when to seek urgent help.

Reassurance for parentsCroup is common in young children and is usually caused by a virus. Most children have a mild illness, but some can develop increasing airway swelling and noisy breathing, especially at night.

What is Croup?

Croup is an infection that causes swelling of the voice box (larynx) and windpipe (trachea). The narrowed airway causes a barking cough, hoarse voice, and sometimes a high-pitched noise when breathing in called stridor.

Symptoms & Signs

  • Barking, seal-like cough
  • Hoarse voice
  • Stridor (a harsh noise when breathing in), often worse when upset
  • Runny nose or fever
  • Symptoms often worsen at night

Causes

  • Usually caused by a viral infection
  • Most common in children aged about 6 months to 6 years
  • Cold air, crying, and agitation may make the noisy breathing more obvious

Home Management

  • Keep your child calm; distress can worsen stridor.
  • Sit your child upright and offer small, frequent sips of fluid.
  • Use simple fever or pain relief only as age-appropriate and as advised on the label or by your doctor.
  • Observe closely overnight if symptoms are mild.
  • Do not use steam or hot vapour, as it is not routinely recommended and can cause burns.

Red Flags / When to Seek Help

  • Stridor present even when your child is resting
  • Fast breathing, chest pulling in, or struggling to breathe
  • Blue lips, pale colour, or your child looks exhausted
  • Drooling, difficulty swallowing, or inability to speak/cry properly
  • Very sleepy, difficult to wake, or not drinking

Important Facts / Myth correction

Most croup is viral, so antibiotics are usually not helpful.
Some children need steroids prescribed by a doctor to reduce airway swelling.
Mild croup can often be managed at home, but breathing difficulty is an emergency.
