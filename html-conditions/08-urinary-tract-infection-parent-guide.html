What is Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)?

A urinary tract infection is an infection anywhere in the urinary tract, including the kidneys, bladder, or urethra.

Symptoms & Signs

  • Fever, especially in younger children
  • Pain or burning when passing urine
  • Passing urine often or urgently
  • Tummy pain, back pain, vomiting, or poor feeding
  • Smelly, cloudy, or blood-stained urine may occur

Causes

  • Bacteria entering the urinary tract
  • More common in children still in nappies
  • Constipation or bladder-emptying problems can contribute
  • Some children need further assessment if UTIs recur

Home Management

  • See a doctor if UTI is suspected.
  • Encourage fluids if your child can drink.
  • Give antibiotics exactly as prescribed if started.
  • Do not delay review for a young child with fever and no clear source.
  • Arrange follow-up if symptoms persist or recur.

Red Flags / When to Seek Help

  • Baby under three months with fever
  • Fever with vomiting or back pain
  • Very unwell, drowsy, or dehydrated child
  • Blood in urine or severe pain
  • Symptoms not improving after treatment starts

Important Facts / Myth correction

A clean urine sample is important for diagnosis.
UTIs are treatable but should not be ignored.
Recurrent UTIs may need further medical review.
