What is Measles?

Measles is a viral illness that causes fever and rash. It spreads easily through coughs, sneezes, direct contact, and shared air in enclosed spaces.

Symptoms & Signs

  • Fever
  • Cough and runny nose
  • Red or sore eyes
  • Tiredness or lethargy
  • Red blotchy rash that often starts on the head and spreads down

Causes

  • Caused by the measles virus
  • Spreads very easily through droplets and airborne exposure
  • Infectious before the rash appears
  • Risk is higher in unimmunised or incompletely immunised people

Home Management

  • Contact your doctor before attending clinic if measles is possible.
  • Keep the child away from others as advised.
  • Offer fluids and rest.
  • Use fever comfort measures as advised.
  • Watch carefully for breathing, neurological, or dehydration symptoms.

Red Flags / When to Seek Help

  • Seizure
  • Struggling to breathe
  • Not waking or not interacting
  • Confusion or memory changes
  • Non-blanching rash, worsening cough, persistent vomiting, or not improving after a week

Important Facts / Myth correction

Vaccination is the best protection against measles.
Measles can cause pneumonia, ear infection, diarrhoea, and rarely brain inflammation.
Antibiotics do not treat measles itself because it is viral.
