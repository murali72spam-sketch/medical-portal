What is Meningitis?

Meningitis is infection and inflammation of the membranes covering the brain and spinal cord. It may be caused by bacteria or viruses; bacterial meningitis can worsen rapidly.

Symptoms & Signs

  • Fever and vomiting
  • Severe headache or irritability
  • Drowsiness or hard to wake
  • Stiff neck or dislike of bright light
  • Seizures or bulging fontanelle in babies

Causes

  • Usually caused by viral or bacterial infection
  • Can occur as a complication of other infections
  • Bacterial meningitis is a medical emergency
  • Vaccination helps prevent some types

Home Management

  • There is no safe home-management-first approach if meningitis is suspected.
  • Seek emergency medical care immediately.
  • Keep your child comfortable while arranging urgent transport.
  • Do not delay care waiting for a rash to appear.
  • Follow hospital treatment and follow-up advice.

Red Flags / When to Seek Help

  • Non-blanching red or purple rash
  • Hard to wake, floppy, or very drowsy child
  • Seizure
  • Stiff neck, photophobia, or severe headache
  • Baby with bulging fontanelle, high-pitched cry, or arching back

Important Facts / Myth correction

Meningitis can worsen quickly.
A rash is not always present, so absence of rash does not rule it out.
Keeping immunisations up to date is important prevention.
