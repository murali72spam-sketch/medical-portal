Catch-up Vaccination Parent Guide

Missed vaccines are common, and a clinician can usually help build a safe catch-up plan.

Do not panic if a vaccine was missed. Missing or delaying vaccines can often be corrected safely, but the catch-up plan must be calculated by a clinician.

What does catch-up vaccination mean?

Catch-up vaccination means planning how to protect a child who has missed one or more vaccines or has an incomplete record. The plan depends on the child's age, previous valid doses, medical conditions, travel, outbreak risk, and current guidance.

Does everything need to be restarted?

Not usually. Many vaccine series do not need to be restarted from the beginning, but this must be checked by a doctor. Parents should not guess from memory or use internet tables to calculate a catch-up plan.

What does the doctor review?

  • Original vaccine cards, photos, scans, hospital records, school records, and previous prescriptions if they contain vaccine entries.
  • The child's current age and previous doses.
  • Whether minimum interval rules and current guidance are satisfied.
  • Medical conditions, immune problems, medicines, allergy history, and travel needs.

Why catch-up must be individualized

Catch-up vaccination can involve different rules depending on the vaccine and child. This should be checked against the latest official guidance at the time of consultation. This page intentionally does not provide catch-up tables or interval algorithms.

Medical disclaimer

General education only This guide is parent education only and is not a catch-up vaccination calculator or decision tool. It does not replace individualized medical advice from a qualified clinician.
