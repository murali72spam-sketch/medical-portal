Travel Vaccination for Children

Planning safer travel by reviewing routine vaccines, destination risks, and special vaccine needs before departure.

Travel vaccine advice depends on the destination and the child. Your child's vaccine plan should be confirmed with your paediatrician or travel medicine clinician using the latest applicable guidance.

Why may travel vaccines be needed?

Travel can expose children to infections that are uncommon at home or more common in the destination area. Risk depends on the country, season, length of stay, rural or urban travel, food and water safety, mosquito exposure, animal exposure, and the child's medical history.

Domestic and international travel

Even travel within India may involve different disease risks depending on region, season, outbreaks, and activities. International travel may also involve entry requirements or certificates. This should be checked against the latest official guidance at the time of consultation.

Plan before travel

  • Arrange a vaccine review well before travel when possible.
  • Bring the child's vaccine card, previous illness history, allergy history, and itinerary.
  • Discuss destination-specific risks, including food and water infections, mosquito-borne infections, animal bites, and outbreak situations.
  • Ask whether routine vaccines are up to date before considering extra travel vaccines.

Yellow fever certificate awareness

Some countries may require proof of yellow fever vaccination or a certificate depending on travel route and current regulations. This guide does not provide country-specific rules. Families should check official destination and transit requirements and consult a qualified travel medicine or paediatric clinician.

Beyond vaccines

Travel safety also includes safe food and water, hand hygiene, mosquito bite prevention, sun protection, road safety, and prompt care for fever or animal bites. Vaccines are important but are only one part of travel health planning.

Medical disclaimer

General education only This guide is parent education only and is not a travel vaccination decision tool. It does not replace individualized medical advice, destination-specific assessment, official entry requirement checks, or consultation with a qualified clinician.
