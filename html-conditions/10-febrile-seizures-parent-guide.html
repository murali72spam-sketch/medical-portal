What is Febrile Seizures?

A febrile seizure is a seizure associated with fever in a young child. During the seizure, the child may lose consciousness and have stiffening, jerking, or twitching movements.

Symptoms & Signs

  • Loss of consciousness
  • Stiff or floppy body
  • Jerking or twitching movements
  • Eyes rolling back
  • May go red or blue in the face or make gurgling sounds

Causes

  • Usually occurs when temperature rises suddenly
  • Often related to a common viral infection
  • Most occur in young children
  • A febrile seizure is not the same as epilepsy

Home Management

  • Stay calm and place your child on their side.
  • Move nearby dangerous objects away.
  • Do not put anything in the mouth.
  • Do not restrain the child.
  • Time the seizure and seek medical advice after the event.

Red Flags / When to Seek Help

  • Seizure lasts more than five minutes
  • Breathing difficulty or blue colour not improving
  • First seizure or child under six months
  • Repeated seizures in the same illness
  • Child does not wake or remains very unwell after the seizure

Important Facts / Myth correction

Febrile seizures are common and usually do not cause long-term harm.
Fever medicines may improve comfort but do not reliably prevent febrile seizures.
Every first seizure should be medically assessed.
