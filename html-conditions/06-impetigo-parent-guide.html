Reassurance for parentsImpetigo is a common bacterial skin infection in children. It spreads easily, but it usually responds well to treatment and good skin hygiene.

What is Impetigo (School Sores)?

Impetigo is a bacterial infection of the top layer of the skin. It causes red sores or blisters that may ooze and form a yellow or honey-coloured crust.

Symptoms & Signs

  • Red sores or small blisters
  • Yellow or honey-coloured crusts
  • Itchy skin lesions
  • Often around the nose, mouth, or on exposed skin
  • May spread to other body parts by scratching

Causes

  • Usually caused by Staphylococcus aureus or Streptococcus bacteria
  • Spreads through close contact, shared towels, and scratching
  • More common when skin is already broken by eczema, insect bites, or scratches

Home Management

  • Keep nails short and discourage scratching.
  • Wash hands often and avoid sharing towels or clothing.
  • Gently remove crusts as advised so treatment can work better.
  • Follow the treatment plan given by your doctor, which may include antibiotic cream or oral antibiotics.
  • Cover sores when practical.

Red Flags / When to Seek Help

  • Rapidly spreading redness or swelling
  • Fever or child appearing unwell
  • Painful deeper skin infection
  • Sores near the eye or on a very young infant
  • No improvement after treatment starts

Important Facts / Myth correction

Impetigo is contagious and often needs medical treatment.
It is not caused by poor parenting or poor hygiene alone.
Good hygiene helps prevent spread to siblings and classmates.
