How to care for a red, sticky eye safely at home.

Reassurance for parentsConjunctivitis is common in young children. Some cases are infectious and spread easily, while others are allergic or irritant-related. Most children recover well with simple care, but eye pain or reduced vision needs urgent review.

What is Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye)?

Conjunctivitis is inflammation of the conjunctiva, the clear membrane that covers the white part of the eye and the inside of the eyelids. It makes the eye look red or pink and may cause discharge.

Symptoms & Signs

  • Red or pink eye
  • Watery or sticky discharge
  • Crusting on the eyelashes
  • Gritty or irritated feeling
  • Itching, especially if allergic

Causes

  • Viral infection
  • Bacterial infection
  • Allergy
  • Irritation from smoke, dust, or other triggers

Home Management

  • Wash hands well and avoid sharing towels or pillows.
  • Gently clean discharge with clean cotton wool or gauze and water.
  • Try to stop your child rubbing the eyes.
  • Do not use leftover eye drops from an earlier illness.
  • If your child uses contact lenses, keep them out until fully better.

Red Flags / When to Seek Help

  • Eye pain or marked light sensitivity
  • Blurred vision or reduced vision
  • Severe eyelid swelling
  • A newborn with a sticky or red eye
  • Symptoms not improving or your child becomes unwell

Important Facts / Myth correction

Not all conjunctivitis needs antibiotic drops.
Infectious conjunctivitis spreads easily, so hand hygiene matters.
Allergic conjunctivitis usually causes more itching than infection.
