What is Osteomyelitis?

Osteomyelitis is a bacterial infection in a bone. It commonly affects bones around the knee, hip, or ankle.

Symptoms & Signs

  • Pain in a bone or limb
  • Limping or refusal to walk/use a limb
  • Fever or feeling unwell
  • Swelling, redness, or warmth over an area
  • Pain may worsen with movement

Causes

  • Usually caused by bacteria reaching the bone
  • May follow a skin infection, cold, or puncture wound
  • Sometimes no clear cause is found
  • Can occur at any age in childhood

Home Management

  • Do not manage suspected osteomyelitis at home without medical review.
  • Seek urgent assessment for fever plus limp or refusal to use a limb.
  • Give comfort and avoid forcing the child to walk.
  • Follow antibiotic and follow-up plans carefully.
  • Return if pain, fever, or function worsens.

Red Flags / When to Seek Help

  • Fever with refusal to walk or use a limb
  • Severe bone or joint pain
  • Swelling, redness, or warmth over bone/joint
  • Child looks very unwell
  • Symptoms worsening or not improving with treatment

Important Facts / Myth correction

Osteomyelitis usually needs antibiotics.
Early treatment helps prevent complications.
Regular follow-up is important even after discharge.
